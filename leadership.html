Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent

Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent is the Pastor of the historic First African Baptist Church of Sharon Hill, Pennsylvania. A native of Augusta, Georgia, Reverend Dent graduated from Voorhees High School in Denmark, South Carolina and obtained a Bachelor of Science Degree in Physical and Health Education from St. Augustine's College in Raleigh, North Carolina. He received his Master's in Urban Education from Antioch Graduate School of Education and his Supervisory Certificate from Temple University. As an educator, he held the position of Instructional Supervisor of Physical and Health Education with the School District of Philadelphia for 20 years before retiring in 1991.

He holds membership in several professional and fraternal organizations, including Kappa Delta Psi Honor Society, the National, State and local Associations for Health, Physical Education and Recreation. He is a life member of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity. Reverend Dent is a member of both the Baptist Minister's Conference and the Missionary Minister's Conference of Philadelphia and vicinity. He currently serves as a member of the Board of Governors at Palmer Seminary and Eastern University.

Reverend Dent has served as Deacon, Acting Chairman of the Board of Trustees, and Administrative Assistant to the former Pastor of Monumental Baptist Church the late Dr. M.M. Peace, Sr. Licensed to preach on August 23, 1981, he was ordained on January 17, 1982. Reverend Dent works closely with various religious and business leaders in Delaware County. He is chairman of the Board of Directors of the Neighbor-to-Neighbor Community Development Corporation, which brings needed services to the Darby Township Community. He supports the Delaware County Juvenile Court's rehabilitation program. He is active with the South East Delco Foundation for Education, and the Delaware County Family Center's Advisory Board. Deeply concerned about health issues affecting African Americans, he works closely with the Heart Association and the American Red Cross to bring health related seminars, conferences and fairs to the community.

A recipient of the prestigious Benjamin E. Mays Fellowship for Ministerial Study, Pastor Dent completed his studies toward a Master of Divinity Degree at Eastern Baptist Theological Seminar in December of 1984. In 1998 Reverend Dent completed studies for the Doctor of Ministry in The Renewal of Church for Mission Program at Eastern Baptist Theological Seminary.

Reverend Dent is married to Beatrice Collins Dent and they are the proud parents of a son, Richard A. Dent III, and a daughter, Jennifer J. Dent and they have been blessed with a daughter-in-law, Karena Dent and grandsons, Jalen, Kamron and Richard, IV.

Reverend Beverly Hudson

Reverend Beverly S. Hudson accepted Jesus Christ and was baptized August 1975 at Mount Hermon Missionary Baptist Church in Bishopville, South Carolina under the leadership of the late Rev. Bert D. Lewis Sr. This is where she gained her biblical foundation and passion to serve the Lord.

Rev. Bev graduated in 1986 from Morris College in Sumter, South Carolina with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Mathematics. Rev. Bev is also a 2007 graduate of Jameson School of Ministry with a Certificate in Biblical Studies and she is currently matriculating in the Master of Divinity Program at Palmer Theological Seminary. Professionally, she is an employee with the Department of Treasury.

Rev. Bev is a member of the historic First African Baptist Church in Darby Township, Pennsylvania where the Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent is the Pastor. In addition to her Ministerial Duties, Rev. Bev also serves in the following ministries: New Members Discipleship Training – Adult and Youth Instructor, the Word of Life Bookstore, Women’s Ministry, Youth Ministry and the Women of Faith Support Ministry.

When she was lost, Rev. Bev sought the Lord, He heard her and delivered her from all of her fears; at this point, she rededicated her life back to Jesus Christ and has not looked back. As she began to study and meditate on the Word of God, she found herself experiencing the deeper things of God. After much prayer and fasting, she began to seek God for His will for her life and the result was her call to preach the Good News of Jesus Christ; she was licensed to preach on June 26, 2011 and ordained on November 4, 2012 by Rev. Dr. Richard A. Dent.

The Lord blessed Rev. Bev with one son, Chad Jonathan Hudson. For such a time as this, she stands on God’s promises and relies on, “Being confident of this, that He who began a good work in us will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus” – Philippians 1:6.

Reverend Gary Williams

Reverend Gary Williams serves as an associate minister under the Pastorate of Reverend Dr. Richard A, Dent and First African Baptist Church. Rev. Williams was ordained as a gospel minister February 18, 1987 by the late Rev. Dr. J. H. Beatty of Holy Cross Baptist Church in Philadelphia, PA. Rev. Williams earned an Associate's Degree from CommunityRev. Gary College of Philadelphia in General Studies, a Bachelor of Science Degree in Urban Ministry Management from Geneva College (C.U.T.S.) program. Currently he is enrolled in the Master of Theology Program (Pastoral Theology) at Andersonville Theological Seminary Camilla, Georgia. He is married to the former Sandra Lee Groll, his wife for over thirty years. They are the parents of five children, and six grandchildren. Rev. Williams is the former pastor of Friendly Friends Baptist Church in West Philadelphia. He served his country faithfully during the Vietnam era, and retired with twenty-two years of service from the U.S. Army PA National Guard. He has a great concern for people and enjoys talking to people. Currently is serves on the Ministerial staff at First African, sings on the Men's Choir and is a part of the Youth Nursing Home Visitation Ministry. Rev Williams is a SEPTA employee where he spreads the gospel message to many of his passengers His motivation in life is to witness to the lost and unsaved the wonderful gospel message of our Lord and Savior Jesus the Christ.

Minister Wendell Jones

Minister Wendell Jones was born and raised in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania where he received his diploma at Overbrook High School in the Philadelphia School District. Minister Jones received Christ as his personal Lord and Savior at the age of twelve. As years went on he took heed to his calling as a preacher of the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ. He pursued his calling by entering into "The Eastern Baptist Theological Seminary" where he received his diploma in Christian Studies in 1999. In 1999 he was ordained as a deacon at the "Word of Life Ministries" under Pastor Angelo Williams, Sr. On November 29, 2002 Minister Jones preached his trial sermon and gave evidence to his calling as a licensed preacher. He then entered into the "Jameson Evangelistic School for the bible in 2003. Minister Jones furthered his education by entering in the "Center of Urban Theological Studies", where he received his associate degree in biblical studies. Minister Jones worked with the youth at his previous church and was involved with the Big Brothers and Big Sisters, Mentor's Association. The Lord led Minister Jones to First African Baptist Church of Darby Township under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Richard A. Dent, Pastor. Minister Jones is currently working with the Youth Discipleship Class, Saturday Bible Study and the B.R.O.T.H.E.R's Mentoring Program at First African. Minister Jones has three children, Shanyra, Kadia and Whitney Jones. His life verse is found in Philippians 1:6, "Being confident of this very thing, that He which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ."

Minister Dennis M. Holloman

Deacon Dennis M. Holloman accepted Jesus Christ as his Lord and Savior on March 3, 1991 and was baptized by Reverend Dr. Richard A Dent at First African Baptist Church, Darby Township, Pennsylvania. He finds joy in serving as an instructor for New Members Discipleship class. He also served in the following ministries: The Ushers Ministry and the Men’s Choir. He also lends a hand to Neighbor-to-Neighbor Food Cupboard. On June 3, 2001, Dennis was ordained as a Deacon by Rev. Dr. Richard A. Dent. In an effort to ensure that he is spiritually refueled, Deacon Holloman enjoys attending weekly prayer service and bible study. He is currently enrolled in the Eastern’s School of Christian Ministry (ESCM) program at Palmer Theological Seminary pursing a Certificate in Biblical Studies. Deacon Holloman enjoys being a servant to God’s people and because of his faithfulness he has been afforded the opportunity to speak at Chosen 300, the Men's Ministry and at home going services. He is gifted with the ability to help the young, new Christians and those seasoned in Christ. Deacon Holloman is a devoted husband to Agnes Elaine Holloman. He is a loving father to his wonderful daughters Marques and Deneen. He has one granddaughter Layla. What’s most important in Deacon Holloman’s life is being a child of God. God heard Deacon Holloman’s faintest cry and that is why he so joyfully shouts, “What a mighty God we serve!”

Minister Kenneth J. D. Wilson

Brother Kenneth J. D. Wilson was born a twin to the late Patricia Wilson and Kent Wilson in Nuremberg, Germany on November 8, 1980. Kenneth and his twin brother Kevin were raised by their grandparents, George (deceased) and Mary Atkings who took them to church and introduced them to the music ministry at an early age. Kenneth was baptized and spiritually cultivated at Second Pilgrim Baptist Church where he served on the youth choir and usher board. He later became a member of the New Way Missionary Baptist Church where he was an active youth leader, Junior Deacon, Usher and Choir Director. After graduating from Saint Joseph’s Preparatory School in 1999, Kenneth went on to earn his Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology from the University of Connecticut and subsequently earned a Master’s in Criminal Justice with a concentration in Behavioral Analysis in 2006. Currently, he is matriculating in the Master of Divinity Program at Palmer Theological Seminary. Professionally, Kenneth is the Assistant Director of the Eva Donaldson School of Music. Driven by the need to mentor young men of all ages, Kenneth is the founder of “A Different Breed of Men”, a non-profit organization. After many years of wrestling with what he knew to be the voice of the Lord, Kenneth accepted the call of ministry on his life in March 2007. As a faithful member of First African Baptist Church, Kenneth continues to prune his gift for ministry as he actively serves in the following ministries: Discipleship training, Marriage Ministry, Men’s Ministry, Chosen 300 and Music Ministry. On May 12, 2012, Kenneth was united in marriage to his long time love Nicole G. Bynum; the Lord blessed them with three children: Tahjae, JaDen and Kai. In his servitude to the Lord, one of Kenneth’s favorite scripture is, “Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: them he also glorified. What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us who can be against us?” - Romans 8:30-31

Minister Sharon White

Minister White is an associate minister of The First African Baptist Church of Darby Township, PA serving under the leadership of Reverend Dr. Richard A. Dent. She is the Director of Sunday School and Children's Church. Prior to her prayerful submission to God’s Divine move to First African, Minister White served faithfully for 27 years at Sharon Baptist Church of Philadelphia, PA, under the leadership of Bishop Keith W. Reed, Sr. While at Sharon Baptist Minister White served in ministry in the following capacities:

  • Director of “Empowerment Hour” (Sunday School) for which she was responsible for the full operation including strategic planning, curriculum design, the hiring, training, and the skill development of all new instructors. She also developed a screening and evaluative process for new instructors and created training programs for all instructors.
  • Instructor in the Sharon Baptist Bible Institute (SBBI), for which she taught the following courses: “Introduction to the Bible”, “How to Study the Bible”, Bad Girls of the Bible” and “The Names of God”.
  • Staff member & instructor for the Women of Grace Women’s Ministry, which also includes teaching courses such as “Developing Your Personal Relationship with The Lord Through His Word”, a 4 part series on intimacy with God and “Single to Serve”.
  • Administrative support to the Pastors for the Associate Ministers’ Ministry.

Minister White received her certification as a Biblical Counselor through The Christian Research and Development Institute in Philadelphia. She also received certificates of completion for Evangelism through Coral Ridge Evangelism Explosion Program as well as The Bible Training for Church Leaders from the Bible Training Center for Pastors (2 1/2 year training program) at Aldan Union Church on January 18, 2015. Professionally, Minister White is a licensed clinical social worker and is the Program Director for geriatric mobile services at NHS Human Services of Delaware County. She holds a BSW from Eastern College and a Masters of Social Service degree from Bryn Mawr College Graduate School of Social Work & Social Research. She serves on numerous professional boards in the aging & mental health field and has conducted numerous workshops and presentations on a variety of topics including cultural diversity, mental health, depression, aging, etc. Minister White is past adjunct professor in the social work department at Eastern University. Her passion is preaching and teaching the Word of God and ministering to His people! Life verse is Lamentations 3:22 & 23, understanding very well that God’s mercies and faithfulness are unmatched!
